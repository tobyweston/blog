topics:
- developer-experience

when_to_use:
- when the topic is about platform engineering, friction, paved roads, or delivery controls
- when arguing that governance should be built into tooling

summary: >
Developer experience is not separate from governance. The shape of delivery tooling influences
behaviour, policy adherence, and the quality of evidence produced by teams.

key_claims:
- Engineers follow the path that the system makes easiest - the path of least resistance.
- Good platform design can make compliant behaviour the natural default.
- Friction in delivery is often a sign of poor governance implementation, not strong governance.

argument_patterns:
- start with developer friction
- reframe the issue as a design problem in the delivery platform
- show how tooling influences behaviour
- argue for controls embedded in paved roads and workflows

example_phrasing:
- Governance is often experienced through developer tooling.
- If the paved road is badly designed, teams will work around it.
- Good controls should feel like part of the platform, not an interruption to it.

guardrails:
- do not romanticise developer freedom
- keep the balance between safety and speed
