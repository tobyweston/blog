name: Trust Lifecycle
slug: trust-lifecycle

topics:
- trust
- governance
- compliance
- software-delivery

when_to_use:
- when the topic is about governance, controls, or delivery assurance
- when a surface process issue is really about trust

summary: >
A simple model for reasoning about software delivery trust over time:
establish trust, verify trust, and maintain trust.

key_claims:
- High-trust delivery systems are designed, not assumed.
- Trust should be supported by evidence, not ceremony alone.
- Controls are most effective when embedded into normal engineering flow.

argument_patterns:
- start with a delivery or governance friction
- reframe the friction as a trust problem
- explain the three stages: establish, verify, maintain
- show how tooling and evidence support each stage
- end with practical implications for engineering teams

example_phrasing:
- The real problem here is often not compliance but trust.
- Trust has to be established before it can be verified. 
- We want to trust our staff - trust but verify.
- Mature systems maintain trust standards continuously.

guardrails:
- do not make it sound abstract or academic
- anchor the framework in delivery practices and evidence
