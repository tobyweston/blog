summary: >
  Regulated organisations typically implement a governance structure that
  traces external legal and regulatory obligations through a hierarchy of
  interpretation, risk identification, policy, standards and finally
  operational controls. This hierarchy provides lineage from the highest
  level obligations down to the controls implemented within systems and
  processes. By maintaining this structure organisations can demonstrate
  why controls exist, how they address specific risks, and how those risks
  relate to regulatory expectations.

key_claims:
- Governance frameworks create clear lineage from regulation to operational controls
- Regulatory obligations must be interpreted before they can be operationalised
- Risk taxonomies help organisations understand what could go wrong
- Policies and frameworks define organisational expectations
- Minimum control standards describe what controls must achieve
- Controls implement those objectives in specific processes and systems
- Evidence and monitoring demonstrate that controls operate effectively
- Independent assurance verifies the effectiveness of governance and controls

argument_patterns:
- Introduce the idea that regulation alone does not directly create operational controls
- Describe the hierarchy that translates regulation into governance and controls
- Explain the responsibilities of the different lines of defence within the structure
- Emphasise traceability between obligations, risks, standards and controls
- Show how organisations measure themselves against this governance framework

example_phrasing:
- "Regulated organisations typically translate regulatory requirements through a hierarchy of governance artefacts."
- "External laws and regulations are interpreted internally before they can be implemented."
- "Risk taxonomies help organisations identify what could go wrong."
- "Policies and frameworks define organisational expectations, while standards define the minimum level of control required."
- "Controls implement these standards within operational processes."
- "Evidence and monitoring demonstrate whether those controls are operating effectively."
- "Independent assurance provides confidence that the governance framework itself is functioning as intended."

guardrails:
- Do not imply that regulation directly defines operational controls
- Maintain separation of responsibilities across the three lines of defence
- Avoid suggesting that second line functions operate controls
- Avoid suggesting that internal audit designs or implements governance frameworks
- Emphasise traceability and lineage between obligations, risks, standards and controls

---

responsibility_model:
  Laws / Rules / Regulations: External authorities
  Regulatory obligations / interpretation: Second Line of Defence (2LoD)
  Risk taxonomy: Second Line of Defence (2LoD)
  Frameworks and policy: Second Line of Defence (2LoD)
  Minimum control standards: Second Line of Defence (2LoD) with support from First Line of Defence (1LoD)
  Controls: First Line of Defence (1LoD)
  Evidence, monitoring and testing: First Line of Defence (1LoD) with oversight by Second Line of Defence (2LoD)
  Oversight and independent assurance: Third Line of Defence (3LoD)
